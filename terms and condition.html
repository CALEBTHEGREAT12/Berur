Price: The price of the car should be clearly stated and agreed upon by both the buyer and the seller. Any additional fees or charges, such as taxes, title and registration fees, and dealer fees, should also be disclosed. Payment: The terms of payment should be agreed upon by both parties, including the down payment, interest rate (if applicable), and payment schedule. Any late payment fees or penalties should also be disclosed. Warranty: The warranty for the car should be clearly stated, including the length of the warranty and what is covered. Some dealerships may offer extended warranties for an additional fee. Returns and cancellations: The terms of returns or cancellations should be clearly stated, including any fees or penalties that may be incurred. Vehicle condition: The condition of the car should be accurately described, including any known defects or issues. The seller should disclose any accidents or repairs that the car has undergone. Delivery: The terms of delivery should be agreed upon by both parties, including the location, date, and method of delivery. Title and registration: The seller should transfer the title and registration of the car to the buyer at the time of sale. Dispute resolution: The terms for resolving any disputes that may arise between the buyer and seller should be clearly stated. It's important to read and understand the terms and conditions before signing any sales agreement. If you have any questions or concerns, don't hesitate to ask the seller or dealership for clarification.
